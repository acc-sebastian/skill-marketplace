# Negotiation Email Snippets

Building blocks for the follow-up email to the counterparty. Assemble only from
findings the user confirmed they want to raise; keep the email shorter than the
report. Match the language of prior correspondence with the counterparty.

## Structure

1. **Opener** — constructive, deal-forward framing.
2. **Grouped asks** — bundle related findings (e.g., all § 2 carve-out points as
   one ask). Lead with the 🔴 items, but never label them "red flags" or reveal
   severity scoring to the counterparty.
3. **Proposed wording** — offer concrete replacement text (from the catalog's
   fallback bank) rather than open-ended objections; concrete text shortens
   negotiations.
4. **Close** — offer a call, name a target date.

## Tone rules

- Firm on 🔴 substance ("we are unable to accept…", "wir können … nicht abbilden"),
  flexible on drafting ("open to alternative wording achieving the same effect").
- Never disclose internal thresholds (penalty heuristics, escalation routes).
- Never characterize the counterparty's draft negatively ("aggressive",
  "one-sided") — describe the change, not the offense.
- Standard framing for house positions: "in line with our group-wide contracting
  standards" / "entspricht unseren konzernweiten Vertragsstandards".
- For § 10/§ 11 items, reference the regulatory driver neutrally: "as a listed
  company we require…" / "als börsenotiertes Unternehmen benötigen wir…";
  "given applicable export control regulations…".

## Example ask block (EN, carve-outs)

> **Definition of Confidential Information (Clause 1):** We would ask to add the
> customary exclusions (information that is public, previously known, independently
> developed, lawfully received from third parties, or required to be disclosed by
> law with prior notice). Proposed wording attached.

## Example close (DE)

> Gerne stimmen wir die Punkte in einem kurzen Call ab — aus unserer Sicht ist eine
> Unterzeichnung bis [Datum] realistisch, wenn wir die genannten Anpassungen
> vornehmen. Anbei ein Markup mit konkreten Formulierungsvorschlägen.
