## Task
1. **Cluster** the raw sources into 3-6 themes (e.g. price movement, regulation, competitor move, supply/demand shift). Drop items with no bearing on the focus area — note how many you dropped and why, in one line.
2. **For each theme, write a block:**
   - **What happened** — the fact, with the specific number/date/event.
   - **Source** — cite every source used (publication/name + date). If two sources disagree, say so instead of silently picking one.
   - **Why it matters** — the concrete implication for the business given the focus area. If you cannot connect it, write "Relevance to the focus area unclear from sources" rather than forcing a link.
3. **If the sources contain price/index data**, produce a compact table: Item | Latest | Prior period | Change (abs/%) | Source.
4. **Headline** (place at the top, above the themes) — one sentence: the single most important development this period and its business implication.
5. **Watch list** — 2-4 bullets: what to monitor next period and the trigger that would make it material (e.g. "if X crosses $Y, review contract hedging").

**Output order:** Headline → theme blocks → price table (if applicable) → watch list → **Sources** (numbered list of everything cited, so the reader can verify).

**Rules:** Never state a number or fact without a source from the input. Never fill a gap with general market knowledge not present in the sources — say what's missing instead. Maximum 500 words excluding the price table and source list.
