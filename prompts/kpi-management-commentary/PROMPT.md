## Task
Write the management commentary:

1. **Headline** — one sentence stating the overall picture for the period (e.g. "Growth on plan, but churn deterioration puts the H2 target at risk.").
2. **Signal, not noise.** Classify every KPI movement:
   - **Material** — beyond normal fluctuation *and* relevant to targets → gets commentary.
   - **Noise** — within normal fluctuation → listed in one collective sentence at the end, no individual commentary.
   State the threshold you applied (e.g. "movements under ±3% vs. prior period treated as noise unless target-relevant").
3. **For each material movement (max 5), write a block:**
   - **What:** the movement with numbers — actual, prior period, target, delta.
   - **Why:** the most plausible driver. Use the business context first; if the data doesn't support a cause, write "Driver unclear — needs analysis" rather than speculating.
   - **So what:** consequence for full-year target or business risk, quantified where possible.
   - **Action:** the follow-up that management should own — a decision, an analysis, or a countermeasure with a suggested deadline.
4. **Outlook** — 2-3 sentences: trajectory for the next period if current drivers persist, and the one metric to watch most closely.

**Style rules:**
- Maximum 400 words. Board members read 30 packs a quarter.
- Every claim tied to a number from the data. No adjectives without evidence ("strong growth" → "+7.7% vs. prior quarter").
- Never mix "why" speculation into the "what" — keep facts and interpretation visibly separate.
- If the data is incomplete for a proper assessment (e.g. no target values), state precisely what is missing at the top before commenting.
