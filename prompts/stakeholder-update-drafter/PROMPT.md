## Task
Draft the email. Requirements:

1. **Subject line** — specific and scannable: project name + the one thing that matters (e.g. "Fleet Rollout — Phase 2 delay contained, decision needed by Fri").
2. **First two sentences** — the recipient must know the overall state (on track / at risk / off track) and the single most important message without reading further.
3. **Body structure:**
   - **Status** — 2-4 bullets: what happened since the last update, factual, with dates and numbers.
   - **Issue & handling** (only if there is bad news) — state the problem plainly in one sentence, then immediately what has been done about it and what the residual impact is. Never bury bad news mid-paragraph, never lead with excuses.
   - **Ask / next step** — exactly what you need from the recipient, by when, and what happens if the decision slips.
4. **Length** — under 220 words. A sponsor reads this on a phone between meetings.
5. **Style rules:**
   - No passive-voice blame constructions ("mistakes were made") — name the cause neutrally.
   - No hedging filler ("I just wanted to quickly...", "as you may know").
   - One concrete date for every commitment or ask. Never "soon" or "asap".
   - Close with the sender's name placeholder `[NAME]`.

After the email, add a two-line **"Why this framing"** note explaining the key wording choice you made for this recipient — so the sender can adapt it consciously instead of copying blindly.
